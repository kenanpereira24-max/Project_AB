The True Story of SnehaJyothi Trust

Trust Beginning Days

Myself T.G. Murthy was working as a farm worker while continuing my education. I lost my father early, and my mother requested the owner of the farm, Shri G. Anand Sir, to raise me and help me become an educated person. Like a father, he took care of me, provided food, clothes, and education. I am forever grateful to him. Even today, I continue to reside on the same farm.

My Profession

I worked in dairying, feeding cows, and milking. I was also involved in silkworm farming—cutting mulberry plants, feeding silkworms, and helping them grow into the pupa stage where the cocoons form. These cocoons were then sold at the Ramanagara Silk Market.

In 2008–2009, I was recognized by the Karnataka Silkworm Organization, Bengaluru, for producing high-quality cocoons. I received a cash award of ₹10,000 for securing second place at the Vidhana Soudha Banquet Hall.

Inspiration Behind the Trust

Because I received education and life support from Shri G. Anand Sir, I felt a deep desire to help poor and orphaned children. I wanted to provide them the same love, care, and opportunity.

With the help of my wife R. Daisey, I started a small hostel in my own house at Thagachaguppe village. My son David T.M trained the children in Martial Arts, while my daughter Jyothi T.M taught them cultural activities like Yakshagana, Bharatanatyam, and dance.

When the trust started in 2010, we had 9 orphan children. Today, by God’s grace, we have expanded to Bengaluru, Ramanagara, and Mysuru under Government registration. With the help of our generous donors, we now provide shelter, food, and education to 69 children.

Our trust has also received several awards from other recognized trusts and the Government.

The Sad Days We Faced

One of our children, 7-year-old Nissan Buddha, suddenly developed G.B.S disorder. We rushed him through 5–6 hospitals. My son David suggested a nerve specialist in Vijayanagar. By then, the child could no longer walk, and we carried him in our arms.

The doctor directed us to Indira Gandhi Children’s Hospital, Jayanagar. The child was in ICU for 7 days and in the general ward for 6 more days. My wife stayed with him for all 15 days, while I waited at the gate and David brought food for us every day. These were the most painful days of our lives.

Thanks to the care of the hospital director, doctors, nurses, and God’s blessings, the child recovered fully.

Objectives of the Trust

To provide children with good food, education, clothing, and shelter.

To raise them as responsible citizens with strong values.

To continue this service forever with dedication.

Together with your help, we can bring a tremendous change in their lives.

Requirements of the Trust

As approved by the Government of Karnataka, 20 guntas of land in Snehanagar, Bethangere Village must be upgraded with a mould-roof hostel and additional buildings. A borewell is required for water supply.

Our Mysuru branch is currently in a rental building. We need to construct our own building and purchase land in the name of the trust.
